What is Toronto Mesh?

A group interested in building a mesh network who met at CivicTechTO in early 2016.

We want to address barriers to internet access in our city, through building community-owned infrastructure with off-the-shelf hardware and open-source tech.

What is this event?

An opportunity to build connections with people interested in community wireless, infrastructure, and alternative networks/internets.

A way to see networks as...
not just digital communications infrastructure, but also arrangements and intersections of social, cultural, and historical structures.

(Technically) Networks

  • allow connected devices, or nodes, to share resources (basically data)
  • are designed around communication and exchange

Image credit (1964) Paul Baran, On distributed communications networks.

Community Networks

  • are the use of networking tech to build networks by, and for, local communities
  • can take many different forms, topologically, organizationally, and otherwise

Kathy installs an antenna for Red Hook wireless

Mesh Networks

  • have nodes (those connected devices from above) which send and recieve data on a network
  • consist of peers connected to each other, are often decentralized, and more resilient
